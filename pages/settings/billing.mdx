## Billing overview

Use the Billing page at `/billing` to see your current plan, track product usage, understand projected charges, and manage your subscription and invoices from a single place.

<Callout kind="info">

Only tenant admins or account owners can access the Billing page and manage subscriptions. If you do not see Billing in Settings, ask an admin to update your role.

</Callout>

The Billing page is organized into the following areas:

- **Trial banner** (if your account is still in a trial)
- **Current plan and limits**
- **Usage cards** for each product (conversations, voice, SMS, email)
- **Overage breakdown** and **projected total**
- **Recent invoices** with PDF downloads
- **Plan comparison** and upgrade/subscribe actions
- **Manage billing portal** entry point for cancel/reactivate and payment details

The UI relies on a billing service behind the scenes for subscription status, plans, usage, invoices, and checkout or billing portal links.

## Trial status and current plan

### Trial banner

When your tenant is in a trial period, a banner appears at the top of the Billing page.

The banner typically shows:

- Remaining trial days or end date
- The plan you will move to after the trial (if already selected)
- A clear **Subscribe** or **Upgrade** button to start a paid subscription before the trial ends

Once your trial ends or you convert to a paid plan, this banner disappears.

### Current plan and limits

Below the trial banner, you see your **current plan summary**. This section includes:

- **Plan name** (for example, Starter, Growth, Enterprise)
- **Billing interval** (for example, monthly, annually)
- **Base price** (amount per billing period, excluding tax)
- **Included usage limits** per billing period for:
  - Conversations
  - Voice minutes or calls
  - SMS messages
  - Email messages

This section is the reference point for how many units of each product you can consume before overages apply.

## Usage and overage breakdown

The Billing page shows detailed usage for the current billing period, with a breakdown by product and clear indicators when you approach or exceed your plan limits.

### Usage cards

Usage is grouped into separate cards, one per channel:

- **Conversations**
- **Voice**
- **SMS**
- **Email**

Each card typically includes:

- **Current period usage** (for example, `7,500 / 10,000` conversations)
- A **progress indicator** that fills as usage increases
- **Overage usage** if you go beyond your included limit
- A timestamp or note indicating **which billing period** the totals apply to

The Billing page keeps usage aligned with your current subscription period rather than calendar dates.

### Overage and projected total

Below or alongside the usage cards, the Billing page shows a **cost breakdown** for the current billing period:

- **Base plan** charge for your current plan
- **Overage amounts** by product (for example, extra conversations, over-limit SMS)
- **Add-ons** if applicable (for example, extra seats or additional features)
- A **projected total** for the period, combining base, overages, and add-ons

This projection helps you estimate your upcoming invoice based on usage so far. Actual charges may vary slightly depending on proration, adjustments, or local taxes applied by the billing provider.

## View usage details

Use the usage area of the Billing page to quickly understand how close you are to plan limits and where overages may occur.

<Steps>

<Step title="Open the Billing page" icon="settings">

- Go to your application Settings.
- Select **Billing** or navigate directly to `/billing`.

</Step>

<Step title="Review overall plan usage" icon="bar-chart">

- Find the **Current plan** section at the top.
- Confirm your **billing period** and **included usage limits** for each product.

</Step>

<Step title="Inspect per-product usage cards" icon="monitor">

- Scroll to the **Usage** section with cards for conversations, voice, SMS, and email.
- For each card, check:
  - Current period usage versus limit
  - Whether usage is **within** the limit, **near** the limit, or **over** the limit
  - Any labels or colors indicating overage

</Step>

<Step title="Check overage breakdown and projected total" icon="bar-chart">

- Look for the **Cost breakdown** area.
- Review:
  - Base plan amount
  - Overage totals per product
  - Any add-on charges
  - The **projected total** for the current period

</Step>

</Steps>

When everything is working, the Billing page updates usage throughout the billing period, and the projected total reflects what you are likely to be charged on the next invoice.

## Invoices and downloads

The lower part of the Billing page lists your **recent invoices** for the tenant.

Each invoice row typically includes:

- **Issue date** (start or end of billing period)
- **Status** (for example, paid, upcoming, past due)
- **Total amount**
- A **Download** button or link to get the invoice PDF

### Download a past invoice

<Steps>

<Step title="Open the invoices list" icon="folder">

- From `/billing`, scroll down to the **Invoices** section.
- Confirm the table has at least one invoice row.

</Step>

<Step title="Find the invoice you need" icon="search">

- Use the date and amount columns to locate the relevant invoice.
- Check the **status** (for example, paid) if you are reconciling payments.

</Step>

<Step title="Download the invoice PDF" icon="download">

- Click the **Download** action for the invoice row.
- Your browser downloads the PDF from the billing service.
- Open the file to view line items, billing details, and tax where applicable.

</Step>

</Steps>

If the billing service has no invoices yet, the Billing page shows a clear **no invoices** message instead of an empty table. This is common for new tenants that have not completed a full billing cycle.

## Subscribe, upgrade, or change plans

The Billing page lets you start a paid subscription from trial, upgrade to a higher plan, or adjust your plan when usage changes.

The application uses a billing service and hosted **checkout** or **billing portal** to securely collect payment details and confirm changes.

### Start a subscription from trial

<Steps>

<Step title="Confirm trial status" icon="info">

- Go to `/billing`.
- Look for the **Trial** banner at the top, including trial days remaining and the plan you will move to.

</Step>

<Step title="Review available plans" icon="bar-chart">

- Scroll down to the **Plans** or **Plan comparison** section.
- Compare plan names, included limits, and base prices.

</Step>

<Step title="Choose a plan and start checkout" icon="play-circle">

- Click **Subscribe**, **Upgrade**, or a similar button on your chosen plan.
- The app redirects to a hosted **checkout** experience provided by the billing service.

</Step>

<Step title="Complete payment details" icon="lock">

- Enter your payment information and billing details.
- Confirm the subscription in the checkout page.
- After the checkout completes, you are redirected back to the application, and the **Current plan** section updates to show your new plan.

</Step>

</Steps>

### Upgrade or downgrade your plan

After you are on a paid plan, you can move between plans from the same Plan comparison section.

<Steps>

<Step title="Open the plan comparison" icon="settings">

- From `/billing`, scroll to the **Plans** or **Compare plans** section.
- Review how each plan changes your included limits.

</Step>

<Step title="Select a new plan" icon="arrow-right">

- Click **Upgrade** on a higher-tier plan to gain more capacity or features.
- Click a lower plan if you want to reduce included usage and base price.
- The app opens a **billing portal** or **plan change** flow with your billing provider.

</Step>

<Step title="Confirm plan change" icon="check-circle">

- Follow the prompts in the hosted billing experience.
- Confirm the plan change and any proration details presented by the billing provider.
- After completion, the **Current plan** and **usage limits** on `/billing` reflect the updated plan.

</Step>

</Steps>

Actual proration, partial-period credits, and tax handling are managed by the underlying billing provider and may vary by region.

## Manage billing portal, cancel, and reactivate

Some actions, such as updating payment methods or canceling a subscription, happen inside a hosted **billing portal**.

From the Billing page, you typically see a button such as **Manage billing** or **Open billing portal**.

### Open the billing portal

<Steps>

<Step title="Locate the billing portal button" icon="settings">

- Go to `/billing`.
- Find the **Manage billing** or **Open billing portal** button near the Current plan section.

</Step>

<Step title="Launch the portal" icon="external-link">

- Click the button to open the hosted billing portal in a new tab or window.
- The portal is provided by the billing service and pre-loaded with your tenant's subscription.

</Step>

<Step title="Update payment and billing details" icon="edit">

- Inside the portal, you can typically:
  - Update payment methods
  - Change billing contact details
  - View full billing history
- Save changes in the portal. Updates sync back to the application via the billing service.

</Step>

</Steps>

### Cancel or reactivate your subscription

<Steps>

<Step title="Start from the billing portal" icon="alert-triangle">

- Open the billing portal from `/billing` as described above.
- Navigate to the **Subscription** section in the portal.

</Step>

<Step title="Cancel your subscription" icon="trash">

- Choose the **Cancel subscription** or **End plan** option in the billing portal.
- Review any details about when access will end.
- Confirm the cancellation.
- The Billing page updates to show your subscription as canceled or scheduled to cancel at period end.

</Step>

<Step title="Reactivate if available" icon="plus">

- If the billing portal presents a **Resume** or **Reactivate** option:
  - Select it to continue your subscription.
  - Confirm the change.
- After reactivation, the **Current plan** section on `/billing` shows an active subscription again.

</Step>

</Steps>

Options for immediate cancellation, end-of-period cancellation, and reactivation depend on how your tenant's billing is configured in the billing provider.

## Loading, errors, and empty states

The Billing page calls the billing service for several pieces of information: subscription, usage for the current period, invoices, and available plans. The UI accounts for temporary loading or missing data.

### Loading state

When you first open `/billing` or refresh the page:

- A **loading skeleton** or placeholders show where plan, usage, and invoice data will appear.
- After the billing service responds, these skeletons are replaced by actual values.

If loading takes longer than expected, verify network connectivity or try again. Persistent loading problems may indicate an issue with the billing service integration.

### No invoices

If your tenant has never been billed or is still in trial:

- The **Invoices** section shows a **no invoices yet** message.
- The invoice table and Download buttons remain hidden until the first invoice is generated.

### General billing issues

If you see unexpected gaps in data, such as a missing current plan or usage cards not updating, confirm:

- You are signed in as a tenant admin or account owner.
- Your subscription is active in the billing portal.
- The time range is within the current billing period.

Contact support if Billing continues to show incomplete or inconsistent information even after refreshing and confirming your subscription in the billing portal.
