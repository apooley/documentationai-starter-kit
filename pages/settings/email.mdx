## What are Email Settings?

The **Email Settings** page (located at **Settings > Email**) is where you configure how Autoch.at handles incoming and outgoing email communications.

By default, Autoch.at can send and receive emails using a generic `@autoch.at` address. However, to maintain brand professionalism and ensure high deliverability, you should connect your own custom domain (e.g., `support@yourcompany.com`).

## Connecting a Custom Domain

To send and receive emails from your own domain, you need to verify ownership and configure your DNS records.

1. Navigate to **Settings > Email**.
2. In the **Sending Domains** section, click **Add Domain**.
3. Enter the domain you want to use (e.g., `yourcompany.com`).
4. Autoch.at will generate a set of DNS records (TXT and CNAME records) that you need to add to your domain registrar (e.g., GoDaddy, Cloudflare, Namecheap).
5. Log in to your registrar, add the records, and return to Autoch.at to click **Verify**.

<Callout kind="info">
**DNS Propagation:** It can take anywhere from a few minutes to 48 hours for DNS changes to propagate across the internet. If verification fails immediately, wait a while and try again.
</Callout>

### DKIM, SPF, and DMARC

The DNS records provided by Autoch.at configure DKIM (DomainKeys Identified Mail) and SPF (Sender Policy Framework) for your domain. These are essential email authentication protocols that prove to receiving servers (like Gmail or Outlook) that Autoch.at is authorized to send emails on your behalf.

Properly configuring these records is critical for ensuring your emails land in your customers' inboxes, not their spam folders.

## Configuring Inbound Email (Forwarding)

Once your domain is verified for sending, you need to route incoming emails to Autoch.at so they appear in your [Conversations Inbox](/pages/conversations/conversations).

You do this by setting up email forwarding in your current email provider (e.g., Google Workspace, Microsoft 365).

1. In Autoch.at (**Settings > Email**), locate your unique **Inbound Email Address** (it will look something like `inbound-12345@autoch.at`).
2. Log in to your email provider's admin console.
3. Set up a forwarding rule that sends all emails received at your support address (e.g., `support@yourcompany.com`) to the Autoch.at inbound address.
4. Send a test email to your support address to verify it appears in the Autoch.at inbox.

## Reply-To Behavior

When an AI assistant or human agent replies to an email from Autoch.at, you can configure how the "From" and "Reply-To" headers appear to the customer.

- **Default Sender Name**: The name that appears in the customer's inbox (e.g., "Autoch.at Support" or "John from Autoch.at").
- **Default Reply-To Address**: The email address the customer will reply to (e.g., `support@yourcompany.com`).

You can customize these settings globally, or override them on a per-assistant basis in the [Assistant Configuration](/pages/ai-config/assistants).

## Email Signatures

You can configure a standard email signature that will be appended to all outgoing emails sent by human agents.

1. Navigate to **Settings > Email**.
2. Locate the **Email Signature** section.
3. Use the rich text editor to create your signature (including your name, title, company logo, and contact information).
4. Click **Save**.

<Callout kind="tip">
**AI Signatures:** AI assistants do not use the global human signature. You should define how the AI signs off in its Persona instructions (e.g., "Always sign off as 'Support Bot, Autoch.at'").
</Callout>
