## What are Workspaces?

A **Workspace** in Autoch.at is a self-contained environment with its own AI assistants, playbooks, training context, channels, and conversations.

If your organization has multiple distinct brands, products, or departments that need to operate independently, you can create a separate workspace for each one. This allows you to:

- Keep conversations, clients, and settings completely separate between teams.
- Assign different users to different workspaces (e.g., the Sales team only sees the Sales workspace, the Support team only sees the Support workspace).
- Apply different branding, AI configurations, and channel settings to each workspace.

All workspaces are managed under a single **Organization** account, giving administrators a unified view of the entire operation.

## Accessing Workspace Settings

To manage your workspaces, navigate to **Settings > Workspaces**.

From this page, you can see a list of all workspaces in your organization, create new ones, and manage the settings for each.

## Creating a New Workspace

1. Navigate to **Settings > Workspaces**.
2. Click **Create Workspace**.
3. Enter a **Name** for the new workspace (e.g., "Acme Corp - Support", "Acme Corp - Sales").
4. Optionally, add a **Description** to clarify the workspace's purpose.
5. Click **Create**.

Once created, the new workspace will be empty. You will need to configure its AI assistants, connect its channels, and invite users to it separately.

## Switching Between Workspaces

If you have access to multiple workspaces, you can switch between them using the **Workspace Switcher** in the top navigation bar of the Autoch.at dashboard.

Clicking the switcher will display a list of all workspaces you have been granted access to. Select the workspace you want to work in, and the entire dashboard will reload with that workspace's data.

## Managing Workspace Members

Each workspace has its own set of members. A user can be a member of multiple workspaces with different roles in each.

To add a user to a specific workspace:
1. Navigate to **Settings > Workspaces** and select the workspace.
2. Go to the **Members** tab.
3. Click **Add Member** and search for the user by name or email.
4. Assign them a role within this workspace (Owner, Admin, Manager, or Agent).

<Callout kind="info">
**Organization-Level Admins**: Users with the Owner role at the organization level have access to all workspaces by default. Workspace-level roles only apply within that specific workspace.
</Callout>

## Workspace vs. Organization Settings

It's important to understand the distinction between settings that apply to the entire organization and settings that are specific to a single workspace.

| Setting | Level |
|---|---|
| Billing and Subscription | Organization |
| Organization Branding Defaults | Organization |
| User Invitations (to the org) | Organization |
| AI Assistants and Playbooks | Workspace |
| Training Context | Workspace |
| Channel Connections (Chat, SMS, Email) | Workspace |
| Conversations and Clients | Workspace |
| Workspace-specific Branding Overrides | Workspace |
