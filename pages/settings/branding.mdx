## What are Branding Settings?

The **Branding** page (located at **Settings > Branding**) allows you to configure the visual identity of your Autoch.at workspace.

These settings ensure that when your AI assistants or human agents interact with customers—whether via the website chat widget, email, or custom shortlinks—the experience feels native to your organization.

## Branding Scope

If your Autoch.at account supports multiple **Workspaces**, you can define branding at two levels:

- **Organization Default**: These settings apply to all workspaces unless explicitly overridden. This is useful for maintaining a consistent corporate identity across different departments or brands.
- **Workspace Override**: You can customize the branding for a specific workspace (e.g., a sub-brand or a specific product line) without affecting the rest of the organization.

Use the **Branding Scope** dropdown at the top of the page to select which level you are editing.

## Core Branding Elements

The primary tab on this page allows you to configure the core visual elements of your brand.

### Company Information

- **Legal Business Name**: Your official corporate name (e.g., "Autochat Inc."). This is often required for compliance reasons in SMS and email communications.
- **Company Name (Display)**: The friendly name shown to customers in the chat widget header and email signatures (e.g., "Autoch.at").
- **Company Phone Number**: Your primary public contact number.
- **Company Website**: The main URL for your business.

### Visual Identity

- **Logo URL**: Provide a link to your company logo (must be a publicly accessible image URL, like `https://example.com/logo.png`). This logo will appear in the chat widget header and in automated email templates.
- **Primary Color**: Select your brand's primary hex color code (e.g., `#20aea5`). This color will be used for buttons, links, and accents within the chat widget and email templates to match your website's design.

After making changes, click **Save Branding** to apply them across your selected scope.

## Custom Shortlinks

The second tab on the Branding page allows you to configure **Custom Shortlinks**.

When Autoch.at sends an SMS message that includes a link (e.g., a link to a playbook form, a calendar booking page, or a payment portal), it automatically shortens the URL to save characters and track clicks.

By default, these links use a generic Autoch.at domain. To maintain brand trust and improve deliverability, you can configure a custom shortlink domain (e.g., `link.yourcompany.com`).

### Setting Up a Custom Domain

1. Navigate to **Settings > Branding** and click the **Custom Shortlinks** tab.
2. Enter the subdomain you wish to use (e.g., `go.yourdomain.com`).
3. Autoch.at will provide you with a set of DNS records (usually a CNAME record).
4. Log in to your domain registrar (e.g., GoDaddy, Cloudflare, Namecheap) and add the provided DNS records.
5. Return to Autoch.at and click **Verify**.

Once verified, all future SMS links generated by your workspace will use your custom branded domain.
