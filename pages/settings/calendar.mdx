## What is the Calendar Integration?

The **Calendar Integration** (located at **Settings > Calendar**) allows you to connect your calendar application to Autoch.at, enabling your AI assistants to book meetings on your behalf during conversations.

When a customer expresses interest in a demo, consultation, or appointment, the AI can check your real-time availability and book the meeting directly in the conversation—without any human intervention.

## Why Connect Your Calendar?

Connecting your calendar transforms your AI assistants from simple question-answering bots into full-fledged scheduling agents. Key benefits include:

- **24/7 Booking**: Customers can book meetings at any time, even outside of business hours, without waiting for a human to respond.
- **Reduced Back-and-Forth**: Eliminate the "What times work for you?" email chains. The AI presents available slots and confirms the booking instantly.
- **Automatic Invites**: Once a meeting is booked, calendar invites are automatically sent to both the customer and the assigned team member.
- **CRM Integration**: Booked meetings can be automatically logged in your connected CRM as activities or deals.

## Supported Calendar Providers

Autoch.at currently supports integration with the following calendar providers:

- **Google Calendar**: Connect your Google Workspace or personal Gmail calendar.
- **Microsoft Outlook / Microsoft 365**: Connect your Outlook or Microsoft 365 calendar.
- **Calendly**: If you already use Calendly for scheduling, you can connect it to Autoch.at so the AI uses your existing Calendly booking pages.

## Connecting Your Calendar

1. Navigate to **Settings > Calendar**.
2. Click **Connect Calendar**.
3. Select your calendar provider.
4. You will be redirected to your provider's authorization page.
5. Log in and grant Autoch.at permission to read your availability and create events.
6. You will be returned to Autoch.at, and your calendar will be listed as connected.

## Configuring Booking Rules

Once your calendar is connected, you can configure the rules that govern how and when meetings can be booked.

- **Meeting Duration**: Define the default length of a meeting (e.g., 30 minutes, 1 hour).
- **Buffer Time**: Add padding before or after meetings to prevent back-to-back bookings (e.g., 15 minutes before and after each meeting).
- **Availability Window**: Define the days and hours when meetings can be booked (e.g., Monday to Friday, 9 AM to 5 PM in your timezone).
- **Advance Notice**: Require a minimum amount of notice before a meeting can be booked (e.g., "No meetings can be booked less than 24 hours in advance").
- **Maximum Future Booking**: Limit how far in advance a meeting can be scheduled (e.g., "No meetings more than 60 days in the future").

## Enabling Booking in Playbooks

To allow an AI assistant to book meetings, you need to include a booking step in its [Playbook](/pages/ai-config/playbooks).

In your playbook instructions, include a step like: "Once the user has confirmed their interest in a demo, offer to book a meeting. Check the calendar for available slots and present 3 options to the user. Once they select a time, confirm the booking and send them a calendar invite."

The AI will use the connected calendar and your configured booking rules to execute this step automatically.
