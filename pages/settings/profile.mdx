## Profile overview

Use the Profile settings page to manage your personal information and account security without affecting other users.

<Callout kind="info">

Changes to your **Name**, **password**, and **multi-factor authentication (MFA)** apply only to your user account. Updating **Business Name** affects tenant-wide branding and the company name shown in the sidebar.

</Callout>

The Profile page includes:

- Personal details: **Name**, **Business Name**, **Email Address**
- Password management: **current password**, **new password** with a strength meter, and **confirm new password**
- Security controls: **multi-factor authentication (MFA)** settings for additional account protection

## Profile fields reference

Use this section as a quick reference for what each field on the Profile page controls.

<ParamField body="name" param-type="string" required="true" show-location="false">
Your personal display name. The app uses this name in menus, notifications, and activity logs to identify you to other users.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="business_name" param-type="string" required="false" show-location="false">
The tenant-wide company or organization name. The app shows this in the left sidebar and uses it when generating slugs for your workspace or public-facing urls.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="email" param-type="string" required="true" show-location="false">
Your primary login and notification email address. The profile form validates this as a valid email format. Depending on your organization policy, you might not be able to change this value yourself.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="current_password" param-type="string" required="true" show-location="false">
Your existing password. Enter this when changing your password so the system can verify your identity.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="new_password" param-type="string" required="false" show-location="false">
The new password you want to use. The password strength meter updates as you type. The new password must be at least 12 characters long.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="confirm_new_password" param-type="string" required="false" show-location="false">
Repeat of the new password. This must exactly match the new password field before the system accepts the change.
</ParamField>

<ParamField body="mfa_settings" param-type="object" required="false" show-location="false">
Configuration for multi-factor authentication, such as enabling or disabling MFA and managing enrolled factors (for example, authenticator app or sms).
</ParamField>

## Update profile details

Edit your personal and business information to keep your account and workspace branding accurate.

<Callout kind="info">

You can always update your **Name**. Editing **Business Name** changes the tenant-wide branding for everyone in your workspace, so coordinate with your team before making major changes.

</Callout>

<Steps>

<Step title="Open your profile" icon="user">

- Go to **Settings** in the main navigation.
- Select **Profile** from the Settings menu.
- Confirm you see the headings for **Profile** and **Security Settings**.

</Step>

<Step title="Edit your personal details" icon="edit">

- In the **Profile** section, update your **Name** field with how you want it to appear in the app.
- Update **Email Address** if your organization allows self-service email changes.
- Review the form for any inline validation messages and correct errors before saving.

</Step>

<Step title="Update business name (tenant-wide)" icon="settings">

- In the **Business Name** field, enter the company or workspace name you want to display in the sidebar.
- Remember that this name is also used for slug generation, which can affect workspace urls and labels.
- Use a clear, recognizable name that your teammates expect to see.

</Step>

<Step title="Save your changes" icon="check-circle">

- Click the **Save** or **Update Profile** button at the bottom of the section.
- Wait for the confirmation indicator or success message.
- Verify that the sidebar now shows your updated **Business Name** and that your **Name** appears correctly in the header or account menu.

</Step>

</Steps>

If the page shows an error banner after you save, review the message, correct the highlighted fields, and try again.

## Change your password

Strengthen your account security by updating your password regularly and avoiding reused credentials.

<Callout kind="alert">

The app enforces a **minimum password length of 12 characters**. Use a mix of words, numbers, and symbols, and avoid reusing passwords from other services.

</Callout>

The password section includes:

- **Current password** (required)
- **New password** (minimum 12 characters, with strength meter)
- **Confirm new password** (must match the new password)

<Steps>

<Step title="Open the password section" icon="lock">

- From **Settings → Profile**, scroll to the **Change Password** section.
- Confirm you can see the helper text that indicates you can leave fields blank to keep your current password.

</Step>

<Step title="Enter your current password" icon="terminal">

- In **Current password**, type your existing password exactly.
- Use the show or hide toggle if available to avoid typos when entering.

</Step>

<Step title="Choose a new password" icon="shield">

- In **New password**, enter a password with at least 12 characters.
- Watch the **strength meter** below the field; adjust your password until it shows a strong rating.
- Combine multiple unrelated words with numbers or punctuation for better security and memorability.

</Step>

<Step title="Confirm the new password" icon="check">

- In **Confirm new password**, retype the exact same password you entered in the new password field.
- Make sure there are no extra spaces or capitalization differences.

</Step>

<Step title="Apply the password change" icon="check-circle">

- Click **Save** or **Update Password**.
- Wait for the success message indicating your password was changed.
- Sign out and sign back in with your new password to verify that it works, especially if you share devices.

</Step>

</Steps>

If you leave the password fields empty and save the page, the app keeps your existing password unchanged.

## Security settings and MFA

Use the **Security Settings** section to manage multi-factor authentication and protect your account from unauthorized access.

<Callout kind="info">

Multi-factor authentication adds an extra step to sign-in, usually involving a one-time code from an authenticator app or sms in addition to your password.

</Callout>

Although the exact options may vary by organization, expect to see controls for:

- Enabling or disabling MFA for your account
- Viewing which factors are currently active
- Regenerating or viewing backup codes if your organization supports them

<Steps>

<Step title="Open Security Settings" icon="shield">

- Navigate to **Settings → Profile**.
- Scroll to the **Security Settings** section labeled **Manage multi-factor authentication and account security**.

</Step>

<Step title="Enable multi-factor authentication" icon="zap">

- Locate the **MFA** or **Two-step verification** control.
- Start the setup flow, typically by scanning a qr code with an authenticator app or confirming your phone number.
- Enter the verification code provided by your second factor to complete enrollment.

</Step>

<Step title="Review and manage factors" icon="settings">

- Confirm that your primary factor (authenticator app or sms) shows as **active**.
- If backup codes or additional factors are available, store them securely.
- Periodically review your MFA settings to remove unused or outdated devices.

</Step>

<Step title="Disable MFA (if allowed)" icon="x-circle">

- If you need to turn off MFA and your organization allows it, use the **Disable** or **Turn off** option in the MFA section.
- Confirm the action when prompted; the app might require your password again.
- Avoid disabling MFA unless you have another security control in place.

</Step>

</Steps>

If your organization enforces MFA, you might not see an option to disable it and must keep at least one factor active.

## Troubleshooting and common issues

Use these tips to resolve common problems when updating your profile, changing your password, or managing MFA.

### Validation errors when saving profile details

If you see an error banner or validation messages:

- Check that **Email Address** is in a valid format.
- Make sure required fields such as **Name** are not empty.
- Correct any highlighted fields, then click **Save** again.

If errors persist and no fields are highlighted, refresh the page and retry. If the problem continues, contact your workspace admin or support team.

### Password mismatch or weak password

When changing your password, you might see errors like:

- **Passwords do not match**: Verify that **New password** and **Confirm new password** are identical, including capitalization and symbols.
- **Password too short**: Ensure the new password has at least **12 characters**.
- **Weak password**: Improve the strength by adding more characters, mixing letters, numbers, and symbols, or using multiple unrelated words.

The app only updates your password after all three password fields pass validation.

### Cannot edit email address

In some organizations, only admins or centralized identity providers control login emails.

<Callout kind="info">

If the **Email Address** field is disabled or you see a message that email changes are restricted, contact your admin or internal support channel to request an update.

</Callout>

Until your email is updated, you continue to sign in and receive notifications at your existing address.

### MFA issues

If you are locked out because of MFA:

- Check that your device time is accurate if you use an authenticator app.
- Try any backup codes or secondary factors provided during enrollment.
- If you still cannot sign in, reach out to your admin or support team to reset your MFA configuration.
