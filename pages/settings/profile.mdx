## Managing Your Personal Account

The **Profile** page (located at **Settings > Profile**) is where you manage the settings specific to your individual user account, rather than the workspace as a whole.

Changes made here only affect your experience in Autoch.at.

## Personal Information

Update the details that identify you to your team and, in some cases, to customers.

- **Full Name**: The name displayed to your teammates in the Conversations Inbox and internal notes. Depending on your workspace's branding settings, this may also be shown to customers during a live chat handoff.
- **Email Address**: The email address you use to log in to Autoch.at. This is also where you will receive system notifications.
- **Avatar/Profile Picture**: Upload a photo to personalize your account. This helps teammates quickly identify your actions in the timeline.

## Notification Preferences

Control how and when Autoch.at alerts you to new activity.

You can customize notifications for different events, such as:
- **New Conversation Assigned to You**: Get an alert when a routing rule or a teammate assigns a conversation directly to you.
- **New Message in Assigned Conversation**: Receive a notification when a customer replies to a thread you own.
- **Mentioned in Internal Note**: Be alerted when a teammate `@mentions` you in a private comment.

For each event type, you can choose your preferred delivery method:
- **Email**: Receive an email notification.
- **Browser Push**: Get a desktop notification (requires you to allow notifications in your browser settings).
- **In-App**: See a badge or alert within the Autoch.at dashboard.

## Security Settings

Manage the security of your Autoch.at account.

### Changing Your Password

If you need to update your password:
1. Navigate to **Settings > Profile**.
2. Scroll down to the **Security** section.
3. Enter your current password.
4. Enter and confirm your new password.
5. Click **Update Password**.

### Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

For enhanced security, we strongly recommend enabling Two-Factor Authentication (2FA). This requires you to enter a code from an authenticator app (like Google Authenticator or Authy) in addition to your password when logging in.

1. In the **Security** section, click **Enable 2FA**.
2. Scan the QR code with your authenticator app.
3. Enter the 6-digit code generated by the app to verify the setup.
4. Save the provided backup codes in a secure location (like a password manager) in case you lose access to your authenticator app.

<Callout kind="warning">
**Workspace Requirements:** Your workspace administrator may require all users to enable 2FA. If this policy is active, you will be prompted to set it up upon your next login.
</Callout>
