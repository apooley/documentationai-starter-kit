## What are Phone Numbers in Autoch.at?

To use Autoch.at's SMS and voice features, you need at least one phone number provisioned within your workspace. These numbers are used to:

- **Receive inbound SMS messages** from customers, which are routed to your AI assistants or the Conversations Inbox.
- **Send outbound SMS messages** via workflows or manual outreach.
- **Receive inbound phone calls**, which are answered by your AI voice assistants.
- **Make outbound calls** (if outbound voice is enabled on your plan).

You can manage your provisioned numbers by navigating to **Settings > Phone Numbers**.

## Provisioning a New Number

To add a new phone number to your workspace:

1. Navigate to **Settings > Phone Numbers**.
2. Click **Provision Number** or **Add Number**.
3. Select the **Number Type**:
   - **Local Number**: A number with a specific area code, ideal for businesses that want to appear local to their customers.
   - **Toll-Free Number**: An 800/888/877 number, ideal for national businesses where customers shouldn't pay for the call.
4. If you selected a Local Number, choose the **Country** and **Area Code** you want.
5. Autoch.at will display a list of available numbers matching your criteria.
6. Select the number you want and click **Provision**.

The number will be immediately available in your workspace and will start appearing as the sender ID for outbound SMS messages.

<Callout kind="info">
**Number Costs**: Each provisioned phone number incurs a monthly fee in addition to your base subscription. The cost varies by number type and country. Review the pricing details in **Settings > Usage & Billing** before provisioning.
</Callout>

## Configuring a Number

After provisioning a number, you can configure how it behaves.

1. Navigate to **Settings > Phone Numbers**.
2. Click on the number you want to configure.
3. Adjust the following settings:

- **Friendly Name**: Give the number an internal label (e.g., "US Support Line", "Sales Hotline") to make it easy to identify.
- **Assigned Assistant**: Choose which AI assistant should handle calls and SMS messages received on this number.
- **Fallback Behavior**: Define what happens if the AI cannot handle the call (e.g., transfer to a specific team, send to voicemail, play a custom message).
- **Business Hours**: Optionally, configure different behavior for calls received outside of your defined business hours.

## Releasing a Number

If you no longer need a phone number, you can release it to stop being charged.

1. Navigate to **Settings > Phone Numbers**.
2. Click on the number you want to release.
3. Click **Release Number**.
4. Confirm the action.

<Callout kind="warning">
**Releasing is permanent.** Once you release a number, it is returned to the carrier's pool and may be assigned to another customer. You cannot recover a released number. Any conversations associated with that number will remain in your history, but the number will no longer be active.
</Callout>

## SMS Compliance (10DLC Registration)

In the United States, sending business text messages requires registration under the **10DLC (10-Digit Long Code)** framework. This is a carrier-level requirement designed to reduce spam and protect consumers.

If you are sending SMS messages in the US, you must register your brand and the specific use cases (called "campaigns") for which you are sending texts.

Autoch.at guides you through this registration process within the Phone Numbers settings page. The registration typically requires:
- Your legal business name and address.
- Your EIN (Employer Identification Number) or business registration number.
- A description of the types of messages you will be sending.

Unregistered numbers may have their messages blocked by carriers. We strongly recommend completing this registration before launching any SMS campaigns.
