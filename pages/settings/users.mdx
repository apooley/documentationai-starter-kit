## Managing Your Team

The **Users** page (located at **Settings > Users**) is where you manage the human agents and administrators who have access to your Autoch.at workspace.

From here, you can invite new team members, change their permissions, and remove users who no longer need access.

## Inviting a New User

To add a teammate to your workspace:

1. Navigate to **Settings > Users**.
2. Click the **Invite User** button.
3. Enter the user's email address.
4. Select the appropriate **Role** for the user (see below for role descriptions).
5. Click **Send Invite**.

The user will receive an email with a link to join your workspace. Until they accept the invitation, their status will show as "Pending."

## User Roles and Permissions

Autoch.at uses a role-based access control (RBAC) system to ensure that users only have access to the features and data they need to do their jobs.

When inviting or editing a user, you must assign them one of the following roles:

### 1. Owner

The Owner has complete control over the workspace. There is typically only one Owner (the person who created the workspace), but ownership can be transferred if necessary.

- **Permissions**: All permissions, including deleting the workspace, managing billing, and transferring ownership.

### 2. Admin

Admins have broad access to configure the platform but cannot manage billing or delete the workspace.

- **Permissions**:
  - Manage all AI Configuration (Assistants, Playbooks, Training Context, Routing).
  - Manage all Settings (Channels, Branding, Integrations).
  - Invite and manage other users (except Owners).
  - View all Analytics and Conversation Intelligence.
  - Access and respond to all conversations in the Inbox.

### 3. Manager

Managers oversee the day-to-day operations of the support or sales team. They can manage conversations and view reports but cannot change core system settings.

- **Permissions**:
  - Access and respond to all conversations in the Inbox.
  - Reassign conversations between agents.
  - View Analytics and Conversation Intelligence.
  - Manage CRM Clients and Connections.
  - *Cannot* modify AI Assistants, Playbooks, or core Settings.

### 4. Agent

Agents are the frontline team members who handle customer interactions. Their access is restricted primarily to the Conversations Inbox.

- **Permissions**:
  - Access and respond to conversations assigned to them or their team.
  - View the CRM profile of the customer they are currently talking to.
  - *Cannot* view workspace-wide Analytics.
  - *Cannot* modify AI Configuration or Settings.

## Editing or Removing Users

As your team changes, you can update user roles or revoke access entirely.

1. Navigate to **Settings > Users**.
2. Locate the user in the list.
3. Click the **Actions** menu (usually represented by three dots) next to their name.
4. Select **Edit Role** to change their permissions, or **Remove User** to revoke their access to the workspace.

<Callout kind="warning">
**Removing a user is permanent.** If you remove a user, they will immediately lose access to the workspace. Any conversations currently assigned to them will be returned to the unassigned queue.
</Callout>
