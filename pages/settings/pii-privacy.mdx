## What is PII & Privacy?

The **PII & Privacy** page (located at **Settings > PII & Privacy**) is where you configure how Autoch.at handles Personally Identifiable Information (PII) and other sensitive data within your conversations.

Autoch.at is designed with privacy in mind, offering tools to automatically detect, redact, and manage sensitive information before it is stored or processed by AI models.

## Data Redaction

Data Redaction is the process of automatically identifying and masking sensitive information in customer messages before they are saved to the database or sent to an AI model for processing.

### How Redaction Works

When a message arrives (via chat, SMS, email, etc.), Autoch.at scans the text for patterns that match common types of PII. If a match is found, the sensitive data is replaced with a placeholder (e.g., `[REDACTED_CREDIT_CARD]`).

This ensures that:
1. Your human agents do not see sensitive information they shouldn't have access to.
2. Sensitive data is not inadvertently included in the context sent to external AI providers (like OpenAI or Anthropic).

### Configuring Redaction Rules

You can customize which types of data Autoch.at should automatically redact.

1. Navigate to **Settings > PII & Privacy**.
2. In the **Data Redaction** section, toggle the switches for the types of PII you want to protect.

Common redaction categories include:
- **Credit Card Numbers**: (e.g., Visa, Mastercard, Amex)
- **Social Security Numbers (SSN)**: (US format)
- **Email Addresses**: (If you don't need to collect them in the conversation body)
- **Phone Numbers**: (If you don't need to collect them in the conversation body)
- **Passwords/Secrets**: (Common patterns for API keys or passwords)

<Callout kind="warning">
**Redaction is irreversible.** Once a message is redacted and saved, the original sensitive data cannot be recovered. Ensure you only redact information you truly do not need for your business processes.
</Callout>

## Data Retention Policies

Data Retention policies dictate how long Autoch.at stores your conversation history and customer data before automatically deleting it.

By default, Autoch.at retains data indefinitely to provide a complete history for your CRM and AI training context. However, many privacy regulations (like GDPR or CCPA) require businesses to delete customer data after a certain period or upon request.

### Configuring Retention

1. Navigate to **Settings > PII & Privacy**.
2. Locate the **Data Retention** section.
3. Select your desired retention period from the dropdown menu (e.g., 30 days, 90 days, 1 year, Indefinitely).
4. Click **Save Settings**.

Once configured, Autoch.at will automatically permanently delete any conversations, attachments, and associated client records that exceed the specified age.

### Handling Deletion Requests (Right to be Forgotten)

If a customer requests that you delete their data (often referred to as the "Right to be Forgotten"), you can manually trigger a deletion from their Client profile.

1. Go to **CRM > Clients**.
2. Search for the customer and open their profile.
3. Click the **Actions** menu (three dots) and select **Delete Client Data**.
4. Confirm the deletion. This will permanently remove their profile and all associated conversation history from Autoch.at.
