## What is the Analytics Dashboard?

The **Analytics** page (located at **Insights > Analytics**) provides a comprehensive view of how your Autoch.at workspace is performing. It aggregates data from all your communication channels and AI assistants to help you understand conversation volume, resolution rates, and the impact of your automation efforts.

Unlike the main Dashboard (which provides a quick operational snapshot), the Analytics page is designed for deeper analysis and reporting.

## Time Range and Exporting

At the top of the Analytics page, you can control the data you are viewing:

- **Time Range Selector**: Choose the period you want to analyze (e.g., Last 7 Days, Last 30 Days, Last 90 Days). All charts and metrics on the page will update to reflect this window.
- **Export**: Click the **Export** button to download the current data as a CSV file for use in external reporting tools or spreadsheets.

## Scheduled Reports

If you need to share performance data with your team or stakeholders regularly, you can configure **Scheduled Reports**.

1. Click **New Schedule**.
2. Define the frequency (e.g., Weekly on Mondays, Monthly on the 1st).
3. Select the metrics you want to include.
4. Enter the email addresses of the recipients.

Autoch.at will automatically generate and email a PDF or CSV report based on your schedule.

## Key Metrics Overview

The top section of the dashboard highlights your most critical performance indicators:

- **Total Conversations**: The absolute number of interactions across all channels during the selected time range.
- **Qualified Leads**: The number of conversations where an AI assistant successfully completed a qualification playbook.
- **Conversion Rate**: The percentage of total conversations that resulted in a qualified lead.
- **Avg Call Duration**: (If Voice is enabled) The average length of phone conversations handled by your AI assistants.

## Advanced Operational Metrics

This section dives deeper into the efficiency and quality of your support and sales operations.

- **First Response Time**: How quickly, on average, a customer receives their first reply (from either an AI or a human).
- **Resolution Time**: The average time it takes to close a conversation from the moment it starts.
- **Agent Response Time**: How quickly human agents reply after a conversation is handed off from an AI assistant.
- **CSAT / Satisfaction**: The average Customer Satisfaction score, derived from post-conversation surveys or AI sentiment analysis.
- **Cost per Qualified Lead**: An estimated metric based on your Autoch.at usage costs divided by the number of qualified leads generated.

## Visualizing Trends

The Analytics page includes several charts to help you spot patterns and trends over time.

### Conversation Volume by Hour (Heatmap)

This heatmap shows when your customers are most active. Darker colors indicate higher conversation volume.

- **Use Case**: Use this data to optimize your human agent staffing schedules. If you see a consistent spike in volume on Tuesday mornings, ensure you have enough agents online to handle escalations.

### Assistant Performance Comparison

If you have multiple AI assistants deployed, this chart compares their performance side-by-side.

- **Use Case**: Identify which assistants are handling the most volume, which have the highest resolution rates, and which are triggering the most handoffs to human agents.

### Conversations Over Time

A line chart showing the daily or weekly trend of total conversations.

- **Use Case**: Track the impact of marketing campaigns, product launches, or seasonal trends on your support volume.

### Channel Distribution

A breakdown of where your conversations are happening (e.g., Chat vs. SMS vs. Email).

- **Use Case**: Understand which channels your customers prefer and allocate resources accordingly.

## FAQ Analytics

This section provides crucial feedback on the effectiveness of your Training Context.

- **FAQ Hits**: The number of times an AI assistant successfully used an FAQ to answer a customer's question.
- **FAQ Misses**: The number of times a customer asked a question that the AI could not answer using the available Training Context.
- **Near-misses**: Instances where the AI found a potentially relevant FAQ but was not confident enough to use it.

**Actionable Insight**: Regularly review your FAQ Misses and Near-misses. These represent gaps in your knowledge base. By adding new FAQs or updating existing ones to cover these topics, you can immediately improve your AI's resolution rate.

## Conversations by Channel

The bottom of the dashboard provides a detailed breakdown of performance metrics for each specific channel (Voice, SMS, Email, Web Chat). This allows you to see if certain channels are driving more qualified leads or requiring more human intervention than others.
