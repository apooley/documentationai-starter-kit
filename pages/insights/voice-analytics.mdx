## What is Voice Analytics?

**Voice Analytics** (located at **Insights > Voice Analytics**) provides a dedicated reporting view for your AI-handled and human-assisted phone calls.

While the main [Analytics](/pages/insights/analytics) dashboard gives you a high-level overview of all channels, Voice Analytics focuses exclusively on the unique metrics relevant to phone interactions, such as call duration, talk time, and transcription quality.

<Callout kind="info">
Voice Analytics is only available if your Autoch.at plan includes voice capabilities and you have at least one phone number provisioned in **Settings > Phone Numbers**.
</Callout>

## Key Voice Metrics

### Call Volume

The total number of inbound and outbound calls handled during the selected time period. You can break this down by:
- **AI-Handled Calls**: Calls where the AI assistant managed the entire interaction without human intervention.
- **Escalated Calls**: Calls that were transferred from the AI to a human agent.
- **Missed Calls**: Calls that were not answered.

### Average Call Duration

The average length of a phone call from the moment it is answered to when it is disconnected. Tracking this metric over time helps you understand if your AI assistants are resolving issues efficiently.

A sudden increase in average call duration might indicate that the AI is struggling with a new type of inquiry and needs updated Training Context.

### AI Containment Rate

The percentage of calls that were fully handled by the AI assistant without requiring a human agent. A high containment rate means your AI is effectively resolving issues autonomously.

### CSAT for Voice

If you have configured post-call surveys (e.g., "Press 1 if you were satisfied with your support"), this metric shows the average satisfaction score for voice interactions.

## Call Transcripts

One of the most powerful features of Voice Analytics is access to AI-generated transcripts of your phone calls.

For each call, you can:
1. **Read the Transcript**: A full text version of the conversation, with speaker labels (e.g., "AI:" and "Customer:").
2. **Listen to the Recording**: Play back the original audio (if recording is enabled and compliant with your local laws).
3. **View AI Insights**: See the AI's analysis of the call, including the detected intent, the playbook that was used, and the final outcome (e.g., "Lead Qualified", "Escalated to Human", "Call Abandoned").

<Callout kind="warning">
**Recording Compliance:** Recording phone calls is subject to strict laws in many jurisdictions (e.g., two-party consent laws in some US states and Canadian provinces). Ensure you have obtained the necessary consent and configured the appropriate disclosures before enabling call recording. Consult your legal counsel if you are unsure.
</Callout>

## Improving Voice Performance

Use the data in Voice Analytics to continuously improve your AI voice assistants.

- **High Escalation Rate**: If many calls are being transferred to humans, review the call transcripts to understand why. The AI may be encountering questions it can't answer—add those topics to your Training Context.
- **Long Call Duration**: If calls are taking too long, review the transcripts for inefficiencies in your Playbook flow. Are there too many clarifying questions? Is the AI repeating itself?
- **Low CSAT**: If customers are dissatisfied after AI-handled calls, review the transcripts for tone and accuracy issues. Refine the assistant's Persona to be more empathetic and direct.
