## Keep your social presence consistent

**Post Queues** (at **Social Media > Queues**) let you define a repeating posting schedule for each of your social accounts. Once you've set up a queue, you just add posts to it and Autoch.at publishes them automatically at the next available time slot.

This is the most efficient way to maintain a consistent social presence without having to manually schedule every individual post.

## How queues work

A queue has two components:

1. **A schedule** — The days and times you want posts to go out (e.g., Monday, Wednesday, and Friday at 10am).
2. **A pool of posts** — The posts waiting to be published.

When a scheduled time slot arrives, Autoch.at takes the next post from the pool and publishes it. If the pool is empty, the time slot is skipped.

## Creating a queue

1. Go to **Social Media > Queues**.
2. Click **Create Queue**.
3. Select the social account this queue is for.
4. Set your posting schedule — choose the days and times you want posts to go out.
5. Click **Save**.

## Adding posts to a queue

There are two ways to add posts to a queue:

- **From the Posts page** — When creating a post, choose **Add to queue** instead of scheduling a specific time.
- **From the Queue page** — Click **Add Post** directly within the queue.

Posts in the queue are published in order (first in, first out). You can drag and drop posts within the queue to reorder them.

## Pausing a queue

If you want to temporarily stop publishing from a queue (for example, during a company crisis or a sensitive news event), click **Pause Queue**. Posts will stay in the queue but won't be published until you resume.

<Callout kind="tip">
It's good practice to pause your queues during major news events or crises, even if your content isn't related. Automated posts that go out at the wrong moment can look tone-deaf.
</Callout>

## Queue analytics

The queue page shows basic stats for each queue — how many posts have been published, the average engagement per post, and the next scheduled publish time.
