## What are Post Queues?

**Post Queues** (located at **Social > Queues**) are automated publishing schedules that allow you to maintain a consistent social media presence without manually scheduling each individual post.

Instead of deciding when each post should go live, you define a recurring schedule (e.g., "Post on Mondays, Wednesdays, and Fridays at 10 AM"), and then add posts to the queue. Autoch.at will automatically publish the next post in the queue at the next available slot in your schedule.

## Why Use Queues?

Queues are ideal for teams that produce content in batches. For example:
- Your content team writes 10 posts on Monday morning.
- They add all 10 posts to the queue.
- Autoch.at automatically distributes them across the week, publishing one post per day at the optimal time.

This approach ensures a steady stream of content without requiring someone to manually schedule each post throughout the week.

## Creating a Queue

To create a new post queue:

1. Navigate to **Social > Queues**.
2. Click **Create Queue**.
3. Give the queue a descriptive name (e.g., "Daily Tips", "Weekly Promotions").
4. Select the **Social Profile(s)** this queue should publish to.
5. Define the **Publishing Schedule** by selecting the days of the week and the times you want posts to go live.
6. Save the queue.

## Adding Posts to a Queue

Once a queue is set up, you can add posts to it in two ways:

1. **From the Post Composer**: When creating a new post in **Social > Posts**, instead of scheduling it for a specific time, select **Add to Queue** and choose the appropriate queue.
2. **From the Queue Page**: Open the queue directly and use the **Add Post** button to create new content specifically for that queue.

## Managing Queue Order

Posts in a queue are published in order, from top to bottom. You can drag and drop posts within the queue to reorder them, ensuring your most important content is published first.

## Pausing and Resuming a Queue

If you need to temporarily stop publishing (e.g., during a company crisis or a major news event where promotional content would be inappropriate), you can **Pause** the queue.

When a queue is paused, no new posts will be published until you **Resume** it. All scheduled posts remain in the queue and will be published in order once the queue is active again.

## Queue Analytics

You can view the performance of the posts published through a specific queue directly from the queue's detail page. This allows you to see which types of content in your queue are generating the most engagement, helping you refine your content strategy over time.

For a broader view of your social media performance, see [Social Analytics](/pages/social/social-analytics).
