## What is the Social Posts Feature?

The **Social Posts** page (located at **Social > Posts**) is a social media publishing tool built directly into Autoch.at. It allows you to create, schedule, and publish content to all your connected social profiles without leaving the platform.

By centralizing your social publishing alongside your customer conversations, you can maintain a consistent brand presence while also managing the incoming responses and DMs that your posts generate.

## Creating a New Post

To create a new social media post:

1. Navigate to **Social > Posts**.
2. Click **Create Post** or **New Post**.
3. In the post composer, select which **Social Profiles** you want to publish to. You can select multiple profiles to publish the same content to several platforms at once.
4. Write your post content in the text area.
5. Optionally, attach images or videos using the media uploader.
6. Choose whether to **Publish Now** or **Schedule** the post for a specific date and time.

<Callout kind="tip">
**Platform-Specific Formatting**: Different social platforms have different requirements (e.g., character limits, image aspect ratios). Autoch.at will warn you if your content exceeds a platform's limits.
</Callout>

## Managing Your Posts

The Social Posts page displays a list of all your posts in different states.

### Post States

- **Scheduled**: Posts that are queued to be published at a future date and time. You can edit or cancel a scheduled post before it goes live.
- **Published**: Posts that have been successfully published to the social platform. You can view their engagement metrics here.
- **Draft**: Posts that you have started but not yet scheduled or published.
- **Failed**: Posts that could not be published due to an error (e.g., the social profile connection expired). You will need to reconnect the profile and retry.

### Editing and Deleting Posts

- **Scheduled Posts**: You can edit the content, change the scheduled time, or delete a post before it is published.
- **Published Posts**: You cannot edit a post after it has been published to a social platform. To make a change, you would need to log in to the platform directly or delete the post and create a new one.
