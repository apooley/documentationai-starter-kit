## What is an AI Assistant?

In Autoch.at, an **Assistant** is the core AI agent that interacts with your customers. You can think of an assistant as a digital employee with a specific role, personality, and set of responsibilities.

You can create multiple assistants for different purposes. For example:
- A **Support Assistant** that answers technical questions and troubleshoots issues.
- A **Sales Assistant** that qualifies leads and books meetings.
- A **Receptionist Assistant** that handles general inquiries and routes conversations to the right department.

## Creating an Assistant

To create a new assistant, navigate to **AI Assistant > Assistants** in the sidebar and click the **Create Assistant** button.

### Basic Configuration

When creating or editing an assistant, you'll need to configure the following basic settings:

- **Name**: A descriptive name for internal use (e.g., "Technical Support Bot").
- **Description**: A brief summary of the assistant's purpose.
- **Persona / System Prompt**: This is the core instruction set that defines how the assistant behaves. It should include the assistant's role, tone of voice, and general guidelines for interacting with customers.

<Callout kind="tip">
**Writing a good persona:** Be specific about the tone. Instead of "Be helpful," try "You are a friendly, concise, and highly technical support engineer. Always ask clarifying questions before providing a solution."
</Callout>

### Channel Assignment

You must assign your assistant to specific communication channels. An assistant will only respond to messages on the channels it is assigned to.

Available channels include:
- **Web Chat**: The chat widget on your website.
- **SMS**: Text messages sent to your provisioned phone numbers.
- **Email**: Emails sent to your connected domains.
- **Voice**: Phone calls made to your provisioned phone numbers.
- **Social**: Messages from connected social media accounts.

<Callout kind="warning">
If multiple assistants are assigned to the same channel, you must configure **Routing Rules** to determine which assistant should handle a given conversation.
</Callout>

## Advanced Configuration

Once the basics are set up, you can fine-tune your assistant's behavior using advanced settings.

### Model Selection

Autoch.at allows you to choose the underlying AI model that powers your assistant. Different models offer varying levels of speed, reasoning capability, and cost.

- **Standard Models**: Good for general inquiries and simple routing.
- **Advanced Models**: Better for complex reasoning, multi-step playbooks, and nuanced technical support.

### Temperature and Creativity

The **Temperature** setting controls how creative or deterministic the assistant's responses are.
- **Low Temperature (e.g., 0.1 - 0.3)**: The assistant will be highly deterministic, sticking closely to the facts and providing consistent answers. Best for support and compliance.
- **High Temperature (e.g., 0.7 - 0.9)**: The assistant will be more creative and conversational. Best for marketing or brainstorming.

### Playbook Attachment

While the persona defines *how* the assistant acts, **Playbooks** define *what* the assistant should achieve. You can attach one or more playbooks to an assistant to guide it through specific conversational flows (e.g., lead qualification, troubleshooting steps).

See the [Playbooks](/pages/ai-config/playbooks) documentation for more details.

## Testing Your Assistant

Before deploying your assistant to live channels, you should test its behavior.

1. Navigate to **Conversations > Messaging > Widget Playground**.
2. Select the assistant you want to test.
3. Interact with the assistant as a customer would.
4. Verify that it follows its persona, uses the correct training context, and executes its assigned playbooks properly.

## Monitoring Performance

Once your assistant is live, you can monitor its performance in the **Insights > Analytics** dashboard. Look for metrics like:
- Total conversations handled
- Resolution rate
- Handoff rate (how often it transfers to a human)
- Customer satisfaction (CSAT) scores

If an assistant is struggling, consider updating its persona, adding more training context, or refining its playbooks.
