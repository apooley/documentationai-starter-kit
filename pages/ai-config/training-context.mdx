## What is Training Context?

**Training Context** is the knowledge base that powers your AI assistants. It's the collection of documents, website links, FAQs, and specific instructions that the AI uses to understand your business, products, and policies.

Without training context, an assistant relies solely on its general knowledge (which may be outdated or incorrect for your specific business). With comprehensive training context, your assistant becomes a specialized expert on your company.

## Types of Training Context

Autoch.at supports several types of training context, allowing you to import knowledge from various sources. Navigate to **AI Assistant > Training Context** to manage your knowledge base.

### 1. Documents

You can upload files directly to Autoch.at. The AI will extract the text and use it to answer customer questions.

- **Supported formats**: PDF, DOCX, TXT, CSV, Markdown.
- **Best for**: Employee handbooks, product manuals, technical specifications, and policy documents.

<Callout kind="tip">
**Keep documents focused:** A 10-page document about a specific product is better than a 500-page document covering everything. Break large manuals into smaller, topic-specific files for better AI retrieval.
</Callout>

### 2. Website Crawling (Links)

You can provide Autoch.at with URLs to crawl and index. The AI will read the text on those pages and use it as context.

- **Best for**: Public-facing knowledge bases, blog posts, pricing pages, and "About Us" sections.
- **Syncing**: You can configure Autoch.at to periodically re-crawl these links to ensure the AI's knowledge stays up-to-date with your website.

### 3. FAQs (Frequently Asked Questions)

FAQs are direct question-and-answer pairs. They are the most precise form of training context because you explicitly define the exact answer the AI should give for a specific question.

- **Best for**: Common customer inquiries (e.g., "What are your business hours?", "Do you offer refunds?").
- **Overrides**: If an FAQ contradicts information found in a document or website link, the AI will prioritize the FAQ answer.

### 4. Specific Instructions (Snippets)

Instructions are short, targeted pieces of text that guide the AI's behavior or provide specific facts that don't fit neatly into a document or FAQ.

- **Best for**: Temporary promotions, specific phrasing requirements (e.g., "Always refer to our product as 'The Platform', never 'The App'"), or handling edge cases.

## Managing Your Knowledge Base

As your business evolves, your training context needs to evolve with it.

### Organizing Context

You can organize your training context using **Tags** or **Folders** (depending on your plan). This allows you to group related documents and links together, making it easier to manage and update them.

### Reviewing AI Usage

You can see which pieces of training context the AI is using most frequently in the **Insights > Analytics** dashboard. If a document is rarely used, it might be outdated or irrelevant. If an FAQ is used constantly, you might want to expand on that topic in your main documentation.

### Updating and Deleting

When a policy changes or a product is updated, you must update the corresponding training context.

1. Go to **AI Assistant > Training Context**.
2. Locate the outdated document, link, or FAQ.
3. Edit the FAQ, re-crawl the link, or upload a new version of the document.
4. Delete any context that is no longer accurate.

<Callout kind="warning">
**Stale context leads to bad answers.** Make it a habit to review your training context regularly, especially after major product releases or policy changes.
</Callout>
