## What are Messaging Directives?

While Playbooks define specific goals and Personas define an individual assistant's tone, **Messaging Directives** are global rules that apply to *all* your AI assistants, regardless of their specific role or the channel they are operating on.

Think of Directives as your company's universal communication guidelines. They ensure consistency and compliance across every automated interaction.

You can manage these global rules by navigating to **AI Assistant > Messaging Directives**.

## Why Use Directives?

Directives are perfect for enforcing rules that should never be broken, such as:

- **Brand Voice**: "Always refer to our company as 'Autoch.at', never 'Auto Chat'."
- **Formatting**: "Always use bullet points when listing more than two items."
- **Compliance**: "Never guarantee a specific delivery date; always say 'estimated delivery'."
- **Safety**: "If a user asks for medical advice, state clearly that we are a software company and cannot provide medical information."

By setting these rules globally, you don't have to remember to add them to every single assistant's persona or every individual playbook.

## Creating a Directive

To create a new global rule:

1. Navigate to **AI Assistant > Messaging Directives**.
2. Click **Create Directive**.
3. Give the directive a clear, descriptive name (e.g., "Formatting: Bullet Points").
4. Write the specific instruction in the **Directive Content** field. Be as clear and unambiguous as possible.
5. Save the directive.

It will immediately take effect for all active assistants.

## Directives vs. Training Context

It's important to understand the difference between a Directive and Training Context (like an FAQ or a Document).

- **Training Context** provides *information* (e.g., "Our office hours are 9 AM to 5 PM EST."). The AI uses this information to answer questions.
- **Messaging Directives** provide *instructions on behavior* (e.g., "Always end your response by asking if there's anything else you can help with."). The AI follows these instructions when formulating its response.

<Callout kind="tip">
**Keep Directives concise.** If you have a 5-page document on your brand voice, don't paste the whole thing into a Directive. Instead, extract the 3-5 most critical rules (e.g., "Never use emojis," "Always use title case for product names") and create individual Directives for them. Upload the full document to Training Context.
</Callout>

## Managing Directives

As your business policies change, you can easily update or disable Directives.

- **Edit**: Modify the content of a directive to refine the AI's behavior.
- **Toggle On/Off**: Temporarily disable a directive without deleting it. This is useful for testing how a rule affects conversation quality or for seasonal guidelines (e.g., "During December, always wish the customer Happy Holidays").
- **Delete**: Permanently remove a directive that is no longer relevant.
