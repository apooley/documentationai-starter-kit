## What are Playbook Experiments?

In Autoch.at, **Experiments** allow you to test different versions of a playbook against each other to see which one performs better. This is essentially A/B testing for your AI assistants.

By running experiments, you can scientifically determine the best way to structure a conversation, ask for information, or handle objections, rather than relying on guesswork.

You can manage your experiments by navigating to **AI Assistant > Experiments**.

## Why Run Experiments?

Experiments are crucial for optimizing your AI's performance. You might want to test:

- **Tone and Phrasing**: Does a casual, friendly greeting result in more engagement than a formal, professional one?
- **Order of Questions**: When qualifying a lead, is it better to ask for their email address first, or their company size first?
- **Length of Responses**: Do users prefer short, concise answers, or detailed, comprehensive explanations?
- **Call to Action (CTA)**: Which phrasing leads to more booked meetings: "Would you like to schedule a demo?" or "Let's get you set up with a product expert."

## Setting Up an Experiment

To run an experiment, you need two different versions of a playbook.

1. **Create your variants**: Go to **AI Assistant > AI Playbooks** and create two versions of the playbook you want to test (e.g., "Lead Qual V1 - Email First" and "Lead Qual V2 - Company Size First").
2. **Navigate to Experiments**: Go to **AI Assistant > Experiments** and click **Create Experiment**.
3. **Select the Playbooks**: Choose the two playbook versions you want to compare.
4. **Define the Goal**: What metric are you trying to improve? (e.g., "Increase the percentage of users who provide their email address").
5. **Set the Traffic Split**: Decide how much traffic each variant should receive. A 50/50 split is standard for A/B testing.
6. **Start the Experiment**: Once configured, launch the experiment. Autoch.at will automatically route incoming conversations to the different playbook variants based on your traffic split.

## Analyzing Results

While an experiment is running, you can monitor its progress in the **Experiments** dashboard.

Autoch.at tracks key metrics for each variant, such as:
- **Completion Rate**: How often did the assistant successfully reach the playbook's goal?
- **Drop-off Rate**: At what step in the playbook did users abandon the conversation?
- **Resolution Time**: How long did it take to complete the playbook?
- **Handoff Rate**: How often did the conversation need to be escalated to a human agent?

### Declaring a Winner

Once you have collected enough data to reach statistical significance, you can declare a winner.

1. Review the metrics for both variants.
2. Identify the variant that performed best against your defined goal.
3. Click **End Experiment**.
4. **Publish the Winner**: Make the winning playbook version the active playbook for your assistant, ensuring all future conversations benefit from the optimized flow.

<Callout kind="tip">
**Test one thing at a time.** If you change the tone, the order of questions, and the CTA all in one variant, you won't know which change caused the improvement (or decline) in performance. Isolate your variables for the most accurate results.
</Callout>
