## How Routing & Handoffs Work Together

The **Routing & Handoffs** page (located at **AI Assistant > Routing & Handoffs**) is where you control the flow of conversations. It consists of two main tabs that work together to ensure customers reach the right destination safely:

1. **Routing Rules (first tab):** Define *when* a conversation should be transferred (e.g., "If the user says 'speak to a human', route to the Support Team").
2. **Handoff Settings (second tab):** Define *how* that transfer happens, including the messages sent to the user and the guardrails that prevent abuse (e.g., "Don't allow more than 3 handoffs per hour").

**The Flow:**
When a message comes in, Autoch.at checks the **Routing Rules**. If a rule matches, it then checks the **Handoff Settings**. If the guardrails allow it, the handoff executes, sending the configured message and transferring the conversation.

---

## Tab 1: Routing Rules

Routing rules act as the traffic cops for your conversations. They evaluate incoming messages and decide if the conversation needs to be moved to a different assistant or escalated to a human team.

### Creating a Routing Rule

1. Navigate to **AI Assistant > Routing & Handoffs**.
2. On the **Routing Rules** tab, click **Create Rule**.
3. Configure the rule's conditions and actions.

### Rule Conditions

You can trigger a routing rule based on several factors:

- **Keywords**: Trigger if the user's message contains specific words (e.g., "human", "agent", "representative", "cancel", "refund").
- **Intent (AI-detected)**: Trigger if the AI determines the user's underlying goal matches a predefined intent (e.g., `billing_issue`, `technical_support`, `sales_inquiry`).
- **Channel**: Apply the rule only to specific channels (e.g., only route SMS messages, keep web chat with the AI).
- **Time of Day**: Route differently during business hours vs. after hours.

### Rule Actions

When a condition is met, the rule executes an action:

- **Route to Human Team**: Escalate the conversation to a specific inbox or group of human agents (e.g., "Tier 2 Support").
- **Route to Another Assistant**: Transfer the conversation to a specialized AI assistant (e.g., move from the "General Greeter" to the "Technical Troubleshooter").

## Tab 2: Handoff Settings

While Routing Rules determine the destination, Handoff Settings control the experience and safety of the transfer.

### Handoff Messaging

When a handoff occurs, you usually want to inform the customer. You can configure different messages based on the destination and the time of day.

- **Business Hours Message**: "I'm transferring you to a human agent now. They usually reply within 5 minutes."
- **After Hours Message**: "Our human agents are currently offline. I've created a ticket, and someone will email you tomorrow morning."
- **Assistant-to-Assistant Message**: "Let me get our technical specialist to help you with that."

### Guardrails (Safety Limits)

Guardrails are crucial for preventing abuse, spam, or endless loops. Even if a Routing Rule matches, a guardrail can block the handoff.

- **Max Handoffs per Conversation**: Prevent a user from constantly requesting transfers back and forth. (e.g., Limit to 2 handoffs per conversation).
- **Cooldown Period**: Prevent a user from triggering a handoff immediately after one just occurred. (e.g., Require a 10-minute wait between handoff requests).
- **Loop Prevention**: Detect if two AI assistants are endlessly transferring a conversation back and forth to each other, and force an escalation to a human or end the conversation.

<Callout kind="warning">
**Guardrails override rules.** If a user triggers a routing rule but has exceeded their max handoffs, the handoff will be blocked, and the AI will send a fallback message (e.g., "I cannot transfer you again at this time. Please wait for an agent to review your case.").
</Callout>
