## The Hybrid Safety System

Autoch.at employs a **Hybrid Safety System** to protect your brand and your customers. This system combines built-in, non-removable system guardrails with your own custom rules.

When evaluating a message (either incoming from a user or outgoing from an AI), the system checks both sets of rules. **The stricter rule always wins.** If the system blocks something, it stays blocked even if you haven't explicitly added it to your custom list. This provides a safety net while allowing you to enforce your specific business policies.

You can configure your custom safety rules by navigating to **AI Assistant > AI Safety**.

## Blocked Keywords

The **Blocked Keywords** section allows you to define specific words or phrases that the AI is strictly forbidden from using in its responses.

This is crucial for preventing the AI from:
- Mentioning competitors by name.
- Using proprietary internal terminology with customers.
- Discussing confidential or unreleased features.
- Using inappropriate or off-brand language.

### Configuring Blocked Keywords

1. Go to **AI Assistant > AI Safety**.
2. In the **Blocked Keywords** text area, enter one keyword or phrase per line.
3. Click **Save Settings**.

If the AI generates a response containing a blocked keyword, the safety system will intercept it, redact the keyword, or block the message entirely and trigger a fallback response (e.g., "I'm sorry, I cannot discuss that topic.").

## Serious Mode Keywords

Not all customer inquiries should be handled with a cheerful, conversational tone. When a customer is upset, threatening legal action, or reporting a critical failure, the AI needs to recognize the severity of the situation and respond appropriately—or escalate immediately.

**Serious Mode** is a state triggered by specific keywords that forces the AI to adopt a professional, empathetic, and highly constrained tone, often prioritizing immediate handoff to a human agent.

### High Confidence Keywords

These keywords trigger Serious Mode immediately, without requiring the AI to verify the context. They are typically unambiguous indicators of a severe issue.

**Examples:**
- `lawsuit`
- `legal action`
- `fraud`
- `theft`
- `cancel my account immediately`

When a High Confidence keyword is detected in a user's message, the AI will instantly switch to Serious Mode and follow your configured escalation protocols (defined in Routing & Handoffs).

### Medium Confidence Keywords

These keywords suggest a potential issue but require the AI to analyze the surrounding context before triggering Serious Mode.

**Examples:**
- `very upset`
- `extremely frustrated`
- `privacy violation`
- `unacceptable`

When a Medium Confidence keyword is detected, the AI evaluates the entire message. If it determines the user is genuinely distressed or reporting a serious problem, it triggers Serious Mode. If the user is simply using the phrase casually (e.g., "I was very upset when my favorite show was canceled, but your product cheered me up!"), the AI will continue normally.

### Configuring Serious Mode

1. Go to **AI Assistant > AI Safety**.
2. Enter your **High Confidence Keywords** (one per line).
3. Enter your **Medium Confidence Keywords** (one per line).
4. Click **Save Settings**.

<Callout kind="tip">
**Combine with Routing Rules:** Ensure you have a Routing Rule set up to handle conversations that enter Serious Mode. You almost always want these escalated to a human agent immediately.
</Callout>
