## What are Qualification Settings?

**Qualification Settings** (located at **CRM > Qualification Settings**) allow you to define the specific criteria that determine whether a lead is considered "qualified" by your AI assistants.

When an AI assistant runs a qualification playbook, it collects information from the customer. Qualification Settings tell the system how to evaluate that information and decide if the lead meets your standards for a sales-ready prospect.

## Why Configure Qualification Settings?

Without defined qualification criteria, your AI assistants have no way to consistently score leads. Two different assistants (or two different conversations) might make different judgments about whether a lead is qualified.

By setting explicit criteria, you ensure:
- **Consistency**: Every lead is evaluated against the same objective standards.
- **Accuracy**: The "Qualified Leads" metric in your Analytics dashboard reflects your actual business definition of a qualified lead.
- **Automation**: Qualified leads can automatically trigger follow-up actions (e.g., assign to a sales rep, send a notification, update the CRM).

## Defining Qualification Criteria

Qualification criteria are the specific data points and thresholds that a lead must meet to be considered qualified.

Common qualification frameworks include:

### BANT (Budget, Authority, Need, Timeline)

BANT is a classic sales qualification framework. A lead is qualified if they have:
- **Budget**: They have the financial resources to purchase your product.
- **Authority**: They are the decision-maker (or a key influencer) for the purchase.
- **Need**: They have a genuine problem that your product solves.
- **Timeline**: They are planning to make a purchase decision within a specific timeframe.

### Custom Criteria

You can also define your own custom qualification criteria based on your specific business model. For example:
- **Company Size**: Only qualify leads from companies with more than 50 employees.
- **Industry**: Only qualify leads from specific industries (e.g., SaaS, Healthcare, Finance).
- **Use Case**: Only qualify leads who have a specific use case that your product supports.

## Configuring Criteria in Autoch.at

1. Navigate to **CRM > Qualification Settings**.
2. Click **Add Criterion**.
3. Define the criterion:
   - **Field**: The data point being evaluated (e.g., "Company Size", "Budget", "Decision Maker").
   - **Condition**: The logic applied to the field (e.g., "is greater than", "is equal to", "contains").
   - **Value**: The threshold or target value (e.g., "50", "$10,000", "Yes").
4. Set the **Weight**: If some criteria are more important than others, you can assign a weight to each one. The system will calculate a weighted score for each lead.
5. Define the **Qualification Threshold**: The minimum score a lead must achieve to be marked as "Qualified."
6. Click **Save Settings**.

## How Qualification Affects Playbooks

Your Qualification Settings work hand-in-hand with your [Playbooks](/pages/ai-config/playbooks). The playbook is responsible for *collecting* the qualification data (by asking the customer the right questions), and the Qualification Settings are responsible for *evaluating* that data.

When a playbook completes, the AI checks the collected data against your Qualification Settings. If the lead meets the threshold, the conversation is tagged as "Qualified Lead" and any configured follow-up actions are triggered.
