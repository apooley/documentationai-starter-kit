## Scope and audience

Autoch.at organizes your workspace into a consistent shell with sidebar navigation, a shared header, and pages that reflect the main areas of your tenant. This overview explains what those areas are, how access works at a user level, and how the Pages docs line up with what you see in the app.

<Callout kind="info">

These docs focus on **tenant workspaces and tenant admins**. Platform-level admin screens for managing multiple tenants are out of scope here.

</Callout>

## Tenants, workspaces, and access

A **tenant** is your organization in Autoch.at. Within a tenant, you may have one or more **workspaces** that separate different brands, regions, or teams.

From a user perspective:

- Your tenant defines **who you share data with** (for example, colleagues in the same company).
- Each workspace defines **which AI assistants, conversations, and settings** you see.
- Your role in a workspace (for example, member, manager, admin) controls what actions you can take.

<Callout kind="info">

If you do not see a workspace or page that a teammate can access, you may lack permissions or not belong to the same workspace. Ask a tenant or workspace admin to review your access.

</Callout>

### Sign-in and email verification

To use tenant pages, you always go through a basic access flow:

- **Sign-in required** – Pages in the main sidebar are only available when you are logged in.
- **Email verification** – Newly invited users may be asked to verify their email before they can use tenant pages.
- **Session timeouts** – If your session expires, the app redirects you back to sign-in when you try to open a tenant page.

In everyday use, this means:

- You can always reach public pages like the login and email verification screens, even if you are not signed in.
- You only see tenant content once you are authenticated and your account is ready.
- If a link sends you to a page but you end up on the sign-in screen instead, log in and try again.

## Common layout conventions

All tenant pages share a common layout so you can move between sections without re-learning the interface.

### Sidebar sections

The left sidebar is your main navigation and maps directly to the structure of this Pages section:

- **Dashboard** – See a high-level snapshot of activity and important shortcuts across your workspace.
- **AI Config** – Manage how your AI assistants behave, connect to data sources, and control automation.
- **Conversations** – View and manage live and historical chat, voice, and email conversations with customers.
- **CRM** – Work with clients and related records that power your conversations and outreach.
- **Insights** – Review analytics, performance metrics, and conversation outcomes across your tenant.
- **Settings** – Configure workspace-wide settings such as members, channels, billing, and preferences.

Each page doc in this section describes one of these areas from a **user workflow** perspective, not from an implementation or developer perspective.

### Header and global controls

The top header bar provides shared tools you will see across most tenant pages:

- **Theme toggle** – Switch between light and dark modes without leaving your current screen.
- **Global search and shortcuts** – Jump quickly to conversations, clients, or pages wherever you are.
- **Onboarding widgets** – See guidance and progress indicators when your tenant is still being set up.
- **Realtime badges and counters** – Get live feedback, such as active conversations, new messages, or pending items.

These controls work the same way in Dashboard, Conversations, CRM, and Insights so you can rely on consistent behavior as you move around.

## How permissions affect what you see

Your role and workspace membership determine which sidebar items and page actions appear.

At a high level:

- **Standard members** focus on day-to-day work in Conversations and CRM.
- **Team leads** often gain extra access in Insights to review performance and outcomes.
- **Tenant admins** can reach more Settings options, including members, channels, and billing.

<Callout kind="info">

If a section described in these docs does not appear in your sidebar, your account may not have access to it yet. Contact a tenant admin to check your role or invite you to the relevant workspace.

</Callout>

## Using the docs alongside the app

Treat this Pages section as a **map between the in-app sidebar and the documentation**. When you open a page in Autoch.at, you can usually find a matching doc that explains:

- What you can do on that page.
- How data and actions on that page affect other areas.
- Which permissions you need for specific tasks.

A practical way to work:

- Keep Autoch.at open in one browser tab and these docs open in another.
- As you explore a sidebar section, open the matching doc and skim the headings.
- Use the doc examples to try actions in your own workspace, then confirm you see similar results.
