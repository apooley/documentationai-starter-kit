## What are Email Workflows?

While Playbooks define the *conversational* goals of an AI assistant, **Email Workflows** (located at **Conversations > Email Workflows**) are designed specifically for the asynchronous nature of email.

Email workflows allow you to automate sequences of emails, manage follow-ups, and handle complex routing based on the content of an incoming email or the status of a lead.

## Key Concepts

To effectively use Email Workflows, you need to understand a few core concepts:

### Triggers

A workflow is initiated by a **Trigger**. This is the event that causes the workflow to start.

Common triggers include:
- **New Email Received**: An email arrives at a specific address (e.g., `support@yourdomain.com`).
- **Tag Added**: A specific tag is applied to a conversation (e.g., "High Priority").
- **Lead Status Changed**: A contact's status in your CRM changes (e.g., from "New" to "Qualified").
- **Time-Based**: A specific amount of time has passed since the last interaction (e.g., "No reply for 48 hours").

### Conditions

Once a workflow is triggered, it evaluates **Conditions** to determine if it should proceed.

Conditions act as filters:
- **Sender Domain**: Only run the workflow if the email comes from a specific domain (e.g., `@acmecorp.com`).
- **Subject Line Contains**: Only run if the subject line includes certain keywords (e.g., "Invoice", "Urgent").
- **Business Hours**: Only run the workflow during (or outside of) your defined business hours.

### Actions

If the conditions are met, the workflow executes one or more **Actions**.

Actions are the steps the workflow takes:
- **Send Email**: Send a pre-written email template or an AI-generated response.
- **Add Tag**: Apply a tag to the conversation for organization.
- **Assign to Team/Agent**: Route the email to a specific human or group.
- **Update CRM**: Change a field in the customer's CRM record.
- **Wait**: Pause the workflow for a specified duration before executing the next action.

## Creating an Email Workflow

1. Navigate to **Conversations > Email Workflows**.
2. Click **Create Workflow**.
3. Give your workflow a descriptive name (e.g., "Support Auto-Responder" or "Sales Follow-up Sequence").
4. Define the **Trigger** that will start the workflow.
5. Add any necessary **Conditions** to filter when the workflow runs.
6. Add the **Actions** you want the workflow to perform.
7. Save and activate the workflow.

## Common Use Cases

### 1. The Auto-Responder

The most basic email workflow is the auto-responder. It acknowledges receipt of an email and sets expectations for a reply.

- **Trigger**: New Email Received (to `support@yourdomain.com`).
- **Condition**: None (applies to all emails).
- **Action**: Send Email (Template: "We received your request and will reply within 24 hours.").

### 2. The Sales Follow-up Sequence

This workflow automates follow-ups for leads who haven't responded.

- **Trigger**: Tag Added ("Demo Requested").
- **Action 1**: Send Email (Template: "Thanks for your interest! Here's a link to book a demo.").
- **Action 2**: Wait (48 hours).
- **Condition**: Has the user replied? (If Yes, exit workflow).
- **Action 3**: Send Email (Template: "Just following up on my previous email. Are you still interested?").

### 3. VIP Routing

This workflow ensures your most important customers get immediate attention.

- **Trigger**: New Email Received.
- **Condition**: Sender Domain is in the "VIP Domains" list.
- **Action 1**: Add Tag ("VIP").
- **Action 2**: Assign to Team ("Tier 3 Support").
- **Action 3**: Send Email (Template: "Hello VIP, we've escalated your request to our senior team.").

## Managing Workflows

As your processes evolve, you can easily update your workflows.

- **Edit**: Modify the triggers, conditions, or actions of an existing workflow.
- **Pause/Activate**: Temporarily disable a workflow without deleting it.
- **Duplicate**: Create a copy of a workflow to use as a starting point for a new one.
- **Delete**: Permanently remove a workflow that is no longer needed.
