## The unified inbox

The **Conversations** page is where your team manages all customer interactions. Whether a message comes in via the chat widget on your website, an SMS text, an email, a phone call, or a social media DM, it lands here in one feed.

You don't need to switch between tools or check multiple inboxes. Everything is in one place, with full context available for every conversation.

## Navigating the inbox

The inbox has three main areas:

- **Left sidebar** — Filters and conversation list. This is where you browse and select conversations.
- **Main panel** — The conversation thread for whichever conversation you've selected.
- **Right panel** — Client details, linked records, and conversation metadata.

### Filtering conversations

At the top of the conversation list, you can filter by:

| Filter | What it shows |
|---|---|
| **Status** | Open, Snoozed, Resolved, or All |
| **Channel** | Chat, SMS, Email, Voice, or Social |
| **Assignee** | Conversations assigned to you, your team, or unassigned |
| **Tags** | Conversations labeled with specific tags |
| **Search** | Full-text search across message content and client names |

Use these filters together to quickly find what you're looking for. For example: "Show me all open, unassigned email conversations tagged as Billing Issue."

## Conversation states

Every conversation has a status that tells you where it is in its lifecycle:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Open** | Active and needs attention |
| **Snoozed** | Temporarily hidden, will reappear at a set time |
| **Resolved** | Closed — the issue was handled |
| **Pending** | Waiting for a response from the customer |

You can change a conversation's status from the conversation detail view or by using the quick actions in the conversation list.

## AI vs. human handling

Autoch.at shows you clearly whether a conversation is currently being handled by an AI assistant or a human agent. Look for the indicator at the top of the conversation thread.

When an AI is handling a conversation, it responds automatically. When a human takes over, the AI steps back. You can take over a conversation at any time by clicking **Take Over** — the AI will stop responding and the conversation will be assigned to you.

## Assigning conversations

Conversations can be assigned to specific agents or left in the shared queue for anyone to pick up.

- **Auto-assignment** — If configured in Settings > Preferences, new conversations are automatically assigned using round-robin or least-busy logic.
- **Manual assignment** — Click the **Assignee** field in the conversation detail to assign it to a specific agent or team.
- **Unassign** — Remove the assignee to return the conversation to the shared queue.

## Bulk actions

You can select multiple conversations in the list and apply actions to all of them at once:

- Resolve all selected conversations
- Assign all to a specific agent
- Apply a tag to all
- Export selected conversations

This is useful for end-of-day cleanup or when a batch of similar issues comes in at once.

## Channel-specific views

The sidebar also has dedicated sections for each channel type, accessible from **Conversations > Messaging**:

- **Chat** — Web chat conversations from your widget
- **SMS** — Text message conversations
- **Email** — Email conversations
- **Voice** — Phone call records and transcripts
- **Widget Playground** — A sandbox for testing your assistant before going live

See the individual channel pages for details on what's unique to each one.
