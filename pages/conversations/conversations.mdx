## What is the Conversations Inbox?

The **Conversations Inbox** (located at **Conversations > Messaging**) is the operational heart of Autoch.at for human agents. It brings every customer interaction—regardless of the channel it originated on—into one centralized, real-time feed.

Instead of switching between an email client, a phone system, a social media management tool, and a live chat dashboard, your team can monitor, manage, and respond to everything from here.

## Navigating the Inbox

The inbox is designed for speed and efficiency, allowing agents to quickly identify conversations that need human attention.

### The Conversation List

The left side of the inbox displays a list of all active and recent conversations. Each item in the list provides a quick summary:

- **Customer Name/Identifier**: Who the conversation is with (e.g., "John Doe", "+15551234567", or an email address).
- **Channel Icon**: A visual indicator of where the conversation is happening (Chat, SMS, Email, Voice, or Social).
- **Preview**: The most recent message sent or received.
- **Status/Tags**: Indicators showing if the conversation is open, closed, or requires human intervention.
- **Timestamp**: When the last activity occurred.

### Filtering and Sorting

You can use the controls at the top of the list to focus on specific types of conversations:

- **Status Filters**: View only "Open" conversations, or review "Closed" ones.
- **Channel Filters**: Isolate conversations from a specific channel (e.g., only show SMS messages).
- **Assignee Filters**: See conversations assigned to you, assigned to a specific team, or currently being handled by an AI assistant.
- **Search**: Quickly find a specific conversation by searching for a customer's name, email, phone number, or keywords within the messages.

## The Conversation Detail View

When you click on a conversation in the list, the full history opens in the main panel. This is the **Conversation Detail** view.

### The Unified Timeline

The timeline displays every interaction in chronological order. This includes:

- **Customer Messages**: What the customer said or asked.
- **AI Responses**: The replies generated by your AI assistants.
- **Human Agent Replies**: Messages sent by your team members.
- **System Events**: Internal notes, status changes (e.g., "Conversation closed"), or handoff events (e.g., "Transferred from Support Bot to Tier 2 Team").

### Taking Over a Conversation

If an AI assistant is currently handling a conversation, but you see that human intervention is needed (or if a Routing Rule has escalated it to your team), you can take over.

1. Open the conversation.
2. Click the **Take Over** or **Assign to Me** button.
3. The AI assistant will pause its responses, and you can begin typing your reply in the composer at the bottom of the screen.

### The Composer

The message composer allows you to send replies directly to the customer on their original channel.

- **Internal Notes**: You can toggle the composer to "Internal Note" mode to leave comments for other agents that the customer will not see.
- **Canned Responses**: Quickly insert pre-written replies for common questions.
- **Attachments**: Send files, images, or documents to the customer.

## Managing Conversation State

To keep the inbox organized, it's important to manage the state of each conversation.

- **Close/Resolve**: When an issue is fully addressed, mark the conversation as "Closed" or "Resolved" to remove it from the active queue.
- **Snooze/Follow-up**: If you are waiting on information from the customer or another team, you can snooze the conversation so it reappears in your inbox at a later time.
- **Reassign**: Transfer the conversation to another agent or team if they are better equipped to handle it.

For a deeper dive into the tools available within a specific thread, see the [Conversation Detail](/pages/conversations/conversation-detail) documentation.
